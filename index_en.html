Location & Geography

Welcome to Klabili Village, a hidden gem in Selemkay District, Tambrauw Regency, Southwest Papua. Geographically, this village is located in the northern part of Papua Island and borders directly with Sorong Regency. Access to Klabili offers its own adventure! You will take a challenging overland journey from Sorong City for 2 hours using a 4x4 vehicle (examples: Hilux, Pajero). The roads that are still dominated by soil and hills promise an epic journey to untouched natural beauty. Klabili Village is surrounded by dense tropical forests, clear rivers, and stunning mountains.

 

Community & Culture

With a population of around 2,500 people, the Klabili community, which is predominantly Moi Tribe, is known to be very friendly and uphold ancestral traditions. They live in harmony with nature, preserving traditional knowledge in farming, hunting, and processing forest products. Local culture is preserved through traditional Moi Tribe dances, cultural studios & houses (SALSES), and beautiful handicrafts such as weaving, noken, and typical Papuan beaded decorations. The Moi Tribe has a strong clan-based social structure, with traditional leaders and clan heads who play important roles. Life in Klabili is greatly influenced by animistic beliefs, where nature and ancestors are honored through various traditional ceremonies.

Activities & Experiences

Various exciting activities you can enjoy in our village

 
Birdwatching in tropical forest

Enjoy an exciting birdwatching experience in Klabili's pristine tropical forest. Here, tourists are accompanied by guides in groups to observe various wild Papuan birds directly.

 
Trekking to explore nature

Explore trekking trails in the forests and hills around Klabili village. Every step offers views of Southwest Papua's green and pristine nature.

 
Camping in the open

Spend an exciting night under the starry sky by setting up tents in Klabili. The quiet atmosphere and fresh night air in the tropical forest make the camping experience even more memorable.

 
Moi Tribe traditional dance

Witness stunning cultural attractions in the form of Moi Tribe traditional dances. Local dancers wear complete Papuan traditional costumes with energetic traditional music.

 
Authentic Papuan local cuisine

Taste various traditional Klabili dishes, such as savory papeda, sagu, taro, cassava, bamboo vegetables, and bamboo snakehead fish processed Papua-style. All these dishes are cooked by village mothers with hereditary recipes.

 
Klabili Waterfall

Visit the enchanting Klabili Waterfall in the middle of the forest. Its clear water splashes and green surroundings provide their own coolness.

 
Nature photo spots

Capture moments at Klabili's beautiful photo spots. There are panoramic hill areas and locations near Klabili Waterfall, perfect for family photos or selfies with stunning natural backgrounds.

 
Local cultural night

Join Klabili's unique cultural night with traditional music performances and communal dining with residents. In this event, visitors experience the excitement of traditional Papuan music and camaraderie before the end of their visit.

 
Bat Cave & Orchid Garden

Explore Klabili's adventurous Bat Cave, then visit the natural orchid garden around it. Here visitors can see bat colonies and various endemic Papuan orchids in their habitat.

 
Homestay & interaction with locals

Stay with local residents in traditional Klabili homestays and feel the warm atmosphere of a Papuan village. Accommodation in the form of residents' houses allows tourists to directly learn Moi culture and village daily life.
